Feelings of detachment or estrangement from others

Emotional detachment, a common reaction to trauma that helps people escape painful negative emotions, is often observed in PTSD patients, characterized by dissociative symptoms such as depersonalization, derealization, and emotional numbing4. These symptoms manifest as a sense of detachment from surroundings, distorted reality, and disconnected thoughts, emotions, and sensations, often emerging during or after traumatic events18.

  • Dissociative symptoms have been associated with complex trauma in children, such as experiences of physical, sexual, and emotional abuse and neglect18.

  • Dissociative symptoms are prevalent in military personnel and veterans diagnosed with PTSD, with studies indicating higher levels of dissociation in those with combat-related PTSD compared to those without2021.
