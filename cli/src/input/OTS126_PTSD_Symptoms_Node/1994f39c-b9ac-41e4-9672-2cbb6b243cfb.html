Sleep disturbance [4]

Emerging evidence highlights insomnia as a core element of PTSD, mechanistically linked to its onset and persistence. Various mechanisms, such as neurobiological changes, impaired fear extinction, disrupted emotional processing during sleep, and repeated exposure to trauma cues during nightmares, contribute to this connection. These mechanisms elucidate how insomnia obstructs natural recovery from trauma, fosters PTSD development, and hampers response to treatments [24].
