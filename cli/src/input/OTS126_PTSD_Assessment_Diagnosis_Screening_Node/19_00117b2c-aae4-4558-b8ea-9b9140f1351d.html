PTSD Checklist for DSM-5 (PCL-5) is a 20-item self-report measure that assesses the 20 DSM-5 symptoms of PTSD.

Purpose:

  • Monitoring symptom change during and after treatment

  • Screening individuals for PTSD

  • Making a provisional PTSD diagnosis

 

There are three formats of the PCL-5:

  • without potentially traumatic event (PTE) assessment (i.e., without Criterion A from the DCM-5)

  • with potentially traumatic event (PTE) assessment

  • with Life Events Checklist for DSM-5 (LEC-5) and PTE assessment

Usage:

  • The PCL-5 is not intended to be used as a stand-alone diagnostic tool.

  • Intended to assess patient symptoms in the past month

  • For a comprehensive diagnosis, clinical interviewing skills and a structured interview (e.g., CAPS-5) are recommended.

  • The checklist can be self-administered by the patient and be completed in approximately 5-10 minutes.

  • A decrease in PCL-5 score ≤28 indicates a clinically significant change
